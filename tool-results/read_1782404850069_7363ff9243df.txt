     1→# 3D Game Assets Research — "Unemployment Simulator 3D"
     2→
     3→A researched list of **specific, downloadable, license-clear assets** for a GTA-style low-poly night-city game built with **Three.js / @react-three/fiber**.
     4→
     5→> **License priority**: CC0 (public domain, no attribution) first, CC-BY (attribution required, easy to display in a credits screen) second.
     6→> **Format priority**: GLTF/GLB (native Three.js) > FBX (loadable via `FBXLoader`) > OBJ (fallback).
     7→> All links below were verified to resolve to the named pack/model page as of this research pass. Always re-check the license text on the download page before shipping — licenses occasionally change.
     8→

    30→## 1. Kenney.nl — CC0 (Public Domain)
    31→
    32→All Kenney assets are released under **CC0 1.0 Universal** — no attribution required, commercial use OK. The **City Kit** series is the backbone of this game. Files ship as `OBJ, FBX, DAE, STL, glTF` — the `glTF` exports drop straight into `useGLTF()` from `@react-three/drei`.
    33→
    34→### City Kit (Suburban)
    35→- **URL**: https://kenney.nl/assets/city-kit-suburban
    36→- **Mirror (itch.io)**: https://kenney-assets.itch.io/city-kit-suburban
    37→- **License**: CC0
    38→- **Formats**: OBJ, FBX, DAE, STL, glTF
    39→- **Contents**: 35+ suburban house models, fences, trees, props — modular footprint tiles
    40→- **Use**: Residential outskirts of the city, low-rent neighborhoods where the player does "shady deals", backyards for robbery missions
    41→
    42→### City Kit (Commercial)
    43→- **URL**: https://kenney.nl/assets/city-kit-commercial
    44→- **OGA mirror**: https://opengameart.org/content/city-kit-commercial
    45→- **License**: CC0
    46→- **Formats**: OBJ, FBX, DAE, STL, glTF
    47→- **Contents**: ~40 commercial buildings (shops, offices, diners) matching the Roads/Suburban footprint
    48→- **Use**: The "downtown" core — stores the player can enter for scam missions, banks for robbery
    49→
    50→### City Kit (Industrial)
    51→- **URL**: https://kenney.nl/assets/city-kit-industrial
    52→- **License**: CC0
    53→- **Formats**: OBJ, FBX, DAE, STL, glTF
    54→- **Contents**: Warehouses, factories, silos, industrial props — fits the other City Kits
    55→- **Use**: Drug-lab / stash-house locations, abandoned warehouse deal spots, back-alley robbery scenes
    56→
    57→### City Kit (Roads)
    58→- **URL**: https://kenney.nl/assets/city-kit-roads
    59→- **itch.io mirror**: https://kenney-assets.itch.io/city-kit-roads
    60→- **License**: CC0
    61→- **Formats**: OBJ, FBX, DAE, STL, glTF
    62→- **Contents**: 65+ modular road tiles — straights, corners, intersections, crosswalks, sidewalks
    63→- **Use**: Snap together the street grid the player walks on. Combine with Quaternius Modular Streets for variety.
    64→
    65→### Poly Pizza bundle (all City Kit models, pre-split per-mesh)
    66→- **URL**: https://poly.pizza/bundle/City-Kit-0CkvGrBJ0u
    67→- **License**: CC0
    68→- **Formats**: FBX, OBJ, glTF (per-asset download)
    69→- **Use**: Easier than the Kenney zip if you only want specific buildings — each model is a separate GLB download ready for `useGLTF`.
    70→
    71→### RPG Urban Pack (2D, for UI/texture reference)
    72→- **URL**: https://www.kenney.nl/assets/rpg-urban-pack

    79→## 2. Quaternius — CC0 (Public Domain)
    80→
    81→All Quaternius packs are **CC0** — free for personal & commercial use, no attribution. Native FBX/OBJ/Blend; many are also re-exported as GLB on Poly Pizza. Quaternius explicitly confirmed glTF exports for the character pack.

   503→## License Compliance Checklist (for the commercial `.exe`)
   504→
   505→1. **CC0 assets** (Kenney, Quaternius, KayKit, OGA-CC0) → ship freely, no attribution. Document them in `CREDITS.md` anyway for goodwill.
   506→2. **CC-BY assets** (some Poly Pizza, some Sketchfab, FMA, White Bat Audio) → display a **Credits screen** reachable from the main menu listing: asset name, author, license, URL. One-time display is legally sufficient; persistent UI is friendlier.
   507→3. **Pixabay assets** → Pixabay license is CC0-like but technically a separate license. Safe for commercial use without attribution. Keep a list anyway.
   508→4. **Avoid** anything tagged:
   509→   - CC-BY-NC (non-commercial)
   510→   - CC-BY-SA (share-alike — viral, forces your whole game's source/art to be CC-BY-SA)
   511→   - "Editorial use only" on Sketchfab
   512→   - GPL-licensed code/audio
   513→5. **Sketchfab** specifically: the download license is set per-upload by the author and can change. Re-verify each model's license badge on the day you ship, and keep a screenshot of the license page in your project's `licenses/` folder as proof.

   517→## Recommended Three.js Integration Notes (no code, just guidance)
   518→
   519→- **Format**: Convert all downloaded FBX → GLB with `npx fbx2gltf` or Blender's export. GLB loads fastest in `useGLTF()` from `@react-three/drei` and supports Draco compression.
   520→- **Draco compression**: Run every GLB through `gltf-transform optimize` + Draco to shrink ~70% — critical for a web/Tauri build.
   521→- **Texture atlases**: Quaternius Downtown City MegaKit ships a single shared atlas — keep it that way, don't split, to preserve the one-draw-call-per-block advantage.
   522→- **Instancing**: Use `<Instances>` / `<InstancedMesh>` from drei for parked cars, street lamps, and pedestrians — 8 car models × 200 instances = one draw call per model.
   523→- **Animations**: Quaternius' Ultimate Characters share a rig with the Universal Animation Library — retarget in Blender once, export a single GLB per character with all needed animations as clips, then play via `useAnimations(actions, ref)`.
   524→- **Lighting the night city**: Street-lamp models (KayKit Post Lantern) should each parent a `pointLight` with warm color (0xffcc88), limited `distance` and `decay=2` for realistic falloff. Set ambient light very low (~0.05) and rely on lamps + building window-emissive materials.
